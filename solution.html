Solutions for overpopulation

Better Education

One of the first measures is to implement policies reflecting social change.
Educating the masses helps them understand the need to have one or two
or two children at most. Similarly, education plays a vital role in understanding
the latest technologies that are making a huge waves in the world of computing.
Families that are facing a hard life and choose to have four or five children
should be discouraged. Open dialogue on abortion and voluntary sterlization
should be seen when talking about overpopulation. A more educated workforce
also makes poverty eradication and economic growth easier to achieve.

Tax Benefits

The government of various countries might have to come up with various
policies relate to tax exemptions to curb overpopulation. One of them
might be to wave a certain part of income tax or lowering rates of income
tax or lowering rates of income tax for those married couples wo have single
or two children. As we humans are more inclined towards money, this may
produce some positive results. A tax benefit comes in different forms, such
as a deduction, exclusion or credit. The amount of tax you can save also
depends on the type of tax benefit you claim because they each offer a
a different form of savings.

Social Marketing

Social marketing has alreday been started by some socities to educate the
public on overpopulation effects. The intervention can be made widespread
at a low cost. A variety of print materials(flyers, brochures, fact sheets,
stickers) an be distributed in places such as local places of worship,
sporting events, local food markets, schools and at car parks. Traditional
commercial marketing aims are primarily financial, though they can have
positive social effects as well. In the context of public health, raise
awareness and induce changes in behaviour. Hence, this an effective solution.
